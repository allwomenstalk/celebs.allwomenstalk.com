Picture this: a young Timothée Chalamet, straddling two worlds-the hustle of New York City and the je ne sais quoi of French culture. 11. Youngest Oscar Nominee in Decades

Imagine being lauded among cinematic greats before you've even hit 25. That's exactly where Timothée Chalamet found himself when he earned an Academy Award nomination for Best Actor at just 22. This wasn't your run-of-the-mill nod; it was a historical one, catapulting him into the spotlight as the youngest nominee in that category since Mickey Rooney in 1939. Let's pause for a second – that's nearly an 80-year gap! Starring in 'Call Me by Your Name,' Chalamet didn't just act; he embodied the character of Elio, securing his place in Hollywood and in the hearts of cinema enthusiasts worldwide.
